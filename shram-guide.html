How to Create Your e-Shram Card Yourself

Get Your e-Shram Card in 5 Minutes—A Step-by-Step DIY Guide.

Registration Steps

4 STEPS
1

Access the Official Portal

expand_more

Visit the official government website and click on register.

touch_app Tap to see details

Go to the official government portal: https://eshram.gov.in. On the homepage, locate the section labeled "Register on e-Shram" and click it to open the self-registration form.

2

Aadhaar Authentication (OTP)

expand_more

Verify your identity using OTP sent to your Aadhaar-linked phone.

touch_app Tap to see details

Enter your Aadhaar-linked mobile number and Captcha. Click "Send OTP". Once verified, you will be prompted to enter your 12-digit Aadhaar number. Agree to the terms and click submit to receive a second OTP for final verification.

3

Fill in Your Profile Details

expand_more

Provide personal, occupation (NCO Code), and bank details.

touch_app Tap to see details

The system will pull your photo and address from Aadhaar. You must manually complete these blocks:

Personal Details: Provide nominee name, marital status, and blood group.
Occupation & Skills: Select your daily work code (e.g. delivery, helper, construction) carefully from the list.
Bank Account: Enter your bank details correctly for future direct government benefits.

4

Preview and Generate

expand_more

Review your details and download your 12-digit UAN Card.

touch_app Tap to see details

Carefully read the preview page to ensure there are no spelling errors. Click "Submit" to generate your 12-digit Universal Account Number (UAN). You can immediately click "Download UAN Card" to save it to your phone.

tips_and_updates Tips for Successful Registration

Don't Forget the NCO Code

If you choose the wrong occupation code, it may affect your eligibility for future welfare schemes. Take your time to pick the right one.

Ensure Bank Accuracy

Double-check your bank account number and IFSC code. If it is incorrect, government direct benefits may not reach your account.

Need Help? (Portal Issues)

If you are facing technical errors (like portal not loading or OTPs not arriving), remember you can visit your nearest Common Service Centre (CSC). They can perform a biometric-assisted registration.
